## PROPOSAL: Campanhã Technology & Research Quarter

### Context

The Campanhã district occupies the eastern gateway of Porto, anchored by the recently
renovated Campanhã Intermodal Terminal and bordered by 12 hectares of underused
rail-adjacent industrial land. The area's current commercial vacancy rate of 34% presents
a clear opportunity for productive redevelopment, particularly given the district's
excellent multimodal transport connections.

### Problem

Porto's technology sector has grown 18% annually since 2021, yet 73% of tech firms
remain concentrated in the Baixa-Boavista corridor. This geographic compression
drives commercial rents upward and leaves eastern districts underserved by high-skill
employment, widening the city's economic divide between west and east.

### Proposed Intervention

We propose converting the former Campanhã rail warehouses into a mixed-use
technology and applied research quarter, modeled on Barcelona's 22@ district
regeneration program. The project comprises three elements:

First, retrofit 8,000 m² of warehouse space into modular laboratory and office units
with shared prototyping facilities, prioritizing energy-efficient building envelopes
rated to Nearly Zero-Energy Building (NZEB) standards.

Second, establish a dedicated fiber-optic backbone connecting the quarter to the
University of Porto's engineering campus, enabling joint research programs and
reducing the friction of talent commuting across the city.

Third, create a 2.5-hectare public green corridor linking the quarter to Parque
Oriental, integrating stormwater management infrastructure with recreational
space — following Copenhagen's Cloudburst Management approach.

### Expected Outcome

Within five years of completion, the quarter is projected to host 40–60 technology
firms employing approximately 1,200 workers, generating an estimated €15M in
annual municipal tax revenue. The project directly addresses Porto's east-west
economic divide while providing the physical infrastructure that applied research
and hardware development demand.
